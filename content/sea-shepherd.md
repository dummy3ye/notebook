The **Sea Shepherd Conservation Society (SSCS)** is one of the world's most famous and controversial marine conservation organizations. Founded on the principle of "aggressive non-violence," it has spent decades operating as a self-appointed "policing" force on the high seas.

## History and Origins

The organization was founded in 1977 by **Captain Paul Watson**, a co-founder of Greenpeace. Watson was famously ousted from Greenpeace's board because his desire for direct action—such as ramming ships—clashed with Greenpeace's strictly pacifist philosophy.

### The Symbolism of the Jolly Roger
Designed by Geert Vons and Paul Watson, the Sea Shepherd logo is a modern take on the pirate flag:
*   **The Skull:** Represents the death humanity brings to the oceans.
*   **Dolphin & Whale:** Symbolizes the goal of restoring ecological balance.
*   **Shepherd's Crook & Trident:** Symbolizes protection and the determination to fight.

## Campaigns and "Whale Wars"

Sea Shepherd gained global mainstream fame through the Animal Planet reality series ***Whale Wars***, which documented their dangerous annual campaigns in the Southern Ocean against the Japanese whaling fleet.

Their tactics included:
*   **Ramming:** Physically disabling illegal vessels.
*   **Prop Foulers:** Dropping ropes to tangle a ship's propellers.
*   **Butyric Acid:** Throwing "stink bombs" onto decks to spoil whale meat.

## Major Controversies

While celebrated by many as heroes of the sea, the group has been labeled as "eco-terrorists" by some governments. In 2013, a U.S. federal appeals court declared Sea Shepherd to be "pirates" in a legal battle, stating, *"You don't need a peg leg or an eye patch to be a pirate."*

## The 2022 Internal Split

In recent years, the organization has undergone a massive internal fracture. Captain Paul Watson was pushed out as the board sought to move toward more "mainstream" methods. This led to the formation of the **Captain Paul Watson Foundation**, which continues the legacy of direct action.

As of 2024, Paul Watson remains in custody in Greenland, facing potential extradition to Japan for his past anti-whaling activities, sparking global "Free Paul Watson" protests.
